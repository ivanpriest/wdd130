Ghana

Ghana is a Sub-saharan African West African country, It is one of the most peaceful countries in the region Ghana, located in West Africa along the Gulf of Guinea, is a country rich in history, culture, and natural resources. Known as the Gold Coast during colonial times, it was a hub for gold, cocoa, and the transatlantic trade. Ghana became the first sub-Saharan African nation to gain independence in 1957, led by Kwame Nkrumah. The country is renowned for its diverse ethnic groups, including the Akan, Mole-Dagbani, Ewe, and Ga-Adangbe, each contributing to its vibrant cultural tapestry. Traditional festivals, music, and dance, like kente weaving and highlife, reflect Ghana's heritage. Celebrated for its hospitality, Ghana is often referred to as the "Gateway to Africa," welcoming visitors with open arms and warm smiles.
